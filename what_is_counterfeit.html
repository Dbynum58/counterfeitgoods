What is Counterfeit?

Counterfeiting is the theft of another person's trademark. Trademarks are used by businesses of all sizes to help consumers like you identify their products. A trademark is often a term, phrase, or symbol that indicates the origin or source of a certain commodity or service sold in commerce.
A counterfeit item is one that exploits another person's brand without their consent. Criminals strive to exploit the reputation of the trademark owner by creating or selling a counterfeit.
